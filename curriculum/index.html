Biology Curriculum

Course Description

Biology is a class designed to teach students the basic biological processes and how they work and interact with each other. This course challenges students to critically think about and examine life in all of its form. Teachers in this course will use a variety of methods to teach students, including; lecture, group discussion, projects, labs, computer assignments, and other unique type of works.

Coursework

Like much of Choice Academy, homework will be strictly optional, but extra credit will be given to each completed assignment. Work in the class will involve worksheets, occasional lectures, and many labs. Grading of labs will be based on following correct procedures and observing the scientific method first, while accuracy and correctness will be considered second. Worksheets will be, for the most part, completion, however it is expected that the material will be known for any examinations and labs.

Grading

Labs (1/3 of total grade):

Labs are group-based and will be graded based on the correct use of the scientific method and performance in the actual lab itself.

Worksheet (1/3 of total grade):

Worksheets and daily work from the textbook will mostly be completion, but students will be responsible for fixing errors. Homework is optional, and will be graded for completion if done.

Quizzes will be held every two weeks, and students are expected to review lectures and worksheets from class. Quizzes may be short answer or multiple choice.

Projects (both individual and group-based) will be counted as daily work, and will be graded based on participation and accuracy.

Major Exams (1/3 of total grade)

3/4 of questions in unit tests will be multiple choice, and 1/4 will be lab practicals, where the student will be tested on identification, procedure, and results from labs done in class. Students are expected to review everything learned from classwork and labs, and should be able to apply the concepts they learned to challenging questions.

Topics

  • To fully understand the processes that allow for life to occur
  • To understand the history of life itself and where life as we know it comes from
  • To understand the chemistry behind cell processes in animals and plants

Subjects

  • Macromolecules
  • Cell Structures
  • Cell Functions (signaling, division, interaction)
  • Energy and Enzymes
  • Photosynthesis
  • Heredity and Evolution

Textbooks

In biology, textbooks will be sparingly used, except for supplement material. Digital textbooks are permitted within the class, but copies will be provided for students who prefer a physical copy.

Elecctronic Policy

Biology follows the general Choice Academy policy, which states that electronics can be freely used during classes except on examination days. It is up to the student to use technology wisely, and is up to the students whether they deem it is necessary to use electronics or not. In the end, the students are responsible for making smart decisions in class in order to ensure their success.


Algebra II Curriculum

Course Description

Algebra II is a course designed to challenge students to critically think and determine how to solve difficult mathematical problems. Application problems will be kept to a minimum just so that students will see the basic applications of mathematics in everyday life, however since the majority of students wonâ€™t have to use trigonometry or find the intersection of functions those types of topics will be taught in a strictly mathematical sense so students can grasp the concepts and use them for when test day comes or to build on for other math classes. Teachers will first lecture students about how to solve certain problems with different processes, and then solve example problems along with them. Then, they will let students solve problems on their own based on the methods learned in lectures.

Coursework

Work in the class will be done in the form of worksheets and textbook problems, and will be graded based on accuracy and work. Homework, on the other hand, is optional and will be graded for completion if done. Students are expected to practice problems went over in class to study for quizzes and tests.

Grading

Classwork (1/4 of total grade):

Classwork will be done in the form of worksheets and textbook problems, and will be graded for accuracy. Homework is optional and will be assigned from problems in the textbook - it will be graded for completion.

Quizzes (1/4 of total grade):

All quizzes will be short answer, and calculators may or may not be allowed, depending on the topic.

Major Exams (3/4 of total grade)

All tests will be half multiple choice and half short answer, and calculators may or may not be used, depending on the topic.

Topics

  • Concepts of Algebra II are used to make students think critically to create solutions for challenging problems.
  • Algebra II combines concepts learned in Geometry and Algebra I and utilizes them together to solve complicated problems.
  • Concepts of Algebra II can be used to develop equations and laws in the real world.

Subjects

  • Manipulating and evaluating functions
  • Complex numbers
  • Polynomial arithmetic and functions
  • Radical and rational functions
  • Exponential and logarithmic functions
  • Exponential growth/decay and Half-life
  • Conics
  • Matrices

Art History Curriculum

Course Description

Art History explores and examines different art forms and their connections throughout history and from every part of the globe. Students will learn how art was created, and how people experienced and responded to that art in a historic context. Within class, students will learn through lecture, student presentations, primary and secondary sources (such as articles), group discussion, and other online resources.
